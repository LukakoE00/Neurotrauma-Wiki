     Antibiotics are the primary way of treating bacterial infections. Certain infections will respond better to specific antibiotics. All antibiotics will cause {{KIDNEY_DAMAGE}} and {{LIVER_DAMAGE}}, and certain antibiotics cause more organ damage than others. Giving antibiotics without enough medical skill will cause further organ damage.

     #### Caveats:

     It is important to note that antibiotics work primarily with the immune system, which means that they do not fight infections themselves, but rather slow down the infection to give the immune system an easier time dealing with it. Stacking different antibiotics will provide greater effect but with diminishing returns.

     #### Antibiotics:

     There are 7 different antibiotics (not including broad-spectrum antibiotics), with their general coverage shown below. Read their pages for more information.

     - {{UNASYN}}
     - {{AUGMENTIN}}
     - {{VANCOMYCIN}}
     - {{GENTAMICIN}}
     - {{CO_TRIMOXAZOLE}}
     - {{PRIMAXIN}}
     - {{CEFTAZIDIME}}

     #### Notes:

     - The percentages refer to how much an antibiotic slows down a certain bacterial strain
     - Antibiotics labelled with Methicillin Sensitive relate to being ineffective against {{MRSA_INFECTION}}
